Raze Privacy Policy Last updated: [06/14/2026] What Raze Collects To provide fitness tracking features, Raze collects and stores: Account information — email address (via Firebase Authentication, including Google Sign-In if used) Profile data — age, sex, height, weight, body measurements, fitness goals Logged data — food entries, workouts, water intake, weight history Step count — read from your device's built-in step sensor (with your permission), used only for display, never shared How Your Data Is Used Your data is used solely to provide the app's features — calculating your calorie targets, tracking your progress, and displaying your history. Raze does not sell your data or share it with third parties for advertising or marketing purposes. Where Your Data Is Stored Your data is stored securely using Google Firebase (Firestore and Firebase Authentication). Firebase's own privacy practices apply to data stored on their infrastructure — see Google's Privacy Policy for details. Food Database When you search for food, Raze queries the USDA FoodData Central and Open Food Facts databases. These searches are not linked to your personal identity. Camera Permission Raze requests camera access only when you choose to scan a barcode or nutrition label. Photos are processed on your device and are not stored or transmitted. Account Deletion You can permanently delete your account and all associated data at any time from Settings → Account → Delete Account. This immediately and permanently removes your data from our systems. Children's Privacy Raze is not directed at children under 13, and we do not knowingly collect data from children under 13. Changes to This Policy We may update this policy as Raze adds new features. Continued use of the app after changes constitutes acceptance of the updated policy.
